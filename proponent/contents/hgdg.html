Welcome Gender and Development System (GAD System)

To ensure the success and approval of your proposals, it is imperative that you familiarize yourself with the Harmonized Gender and Development Guidelines (HGDG). These guidelines serve as the foundation for evaluating projects and initiatives within our institution, with a particular focus on gender equality and inclusivity.

Crucial Steps and Reminders

  1. Read the HGDG thoroughly: Before drafting or submitting any proposal, carefully review the Harmonized Gender and Development Guidelines. Understanding these guidelines will not only align your proposal with institutional priorities but also enhance its potential for approval.
  2. Incorporate Gender Perspectives: Integrate gender perspectives into your project design, objectives, methodologies, and expected outcomes. The HGDG emphasizes the importance of promoting gender equality and addressing the specific needs of diverse groups within our community.
  3. Address Gender Mainstreaming: Ensure that your proposal incorporates gender mainstreaming principles. This involves analyzing the differential impacts of your project on various genders and actively working to minimize gender inequalities.
  4. Highlight Gender-Sensitive Indicators: When outlining project indicators and evaluation criteria, prioritize those that are gender-sensitive. This demonstrates a commitment to assessing the project's impact through a gender lens and contributes to more accurate monitoring and evaluation.
  5. Submit with Confidence: Once you have aligned your proposal with the HGDG and integrated gender considerations effectively, submit your proposal with confidence. Your thorough understanding and application of these guidelines will significantly enhance your proposal's approval rate.

Remember, adherence to the Harmonized Gender and Development Guidelines is not only a requirement but also a reflection of our commitment to promoting gender equality and inclusivity within USeP. By incorporating these guidelines into your proposals, you contribute to building a more equitable and progressive academic environment.

Thank you for your attention to this matter. For any clarification or assistance regarding the HGDG, do not hesitate to reach out to the Gender and Development Office.
